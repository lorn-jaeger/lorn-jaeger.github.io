Multi-scale Planning

I use multi-scale planning to achieve my goals. I plan on the yearly, quarterly, weekly, and daily timescales. The yearly plan comes from my goals for my ideal life, giving an actionable long term, non-trivial target every year. The quarterly plan take the yearly plan and breaks it into phases, and takes into account the seasonality of obligations during the year. The weekly plan gets even more granular, taking the quarterly plan and setting a distinct target, as well as planning for the miscellaneous other tasks that have to do with the day to day operation of my life. The daily plan takes this weekly plan and distills each of the to-do’s into a daily task list, with time blocks throughout the day to get these tasks done.

When do I make these plans?

My ideas of an ideal life will evolve over time and I will change them over time. These plans will not be changed on a schedule.

The yearly plan will be made on my birthday every year.

The quarterly plan will be made at the end of the fall semester, end of the spring semester, and late summer.

The weekly plan will be made each Sunday morning.

The daily plan gets made in the morning daily. However no plans on weekends.

Full Capture Task Management

Daily task management will be heavily integrated into my daily plans. The task list is split into two parts. First is the digital, for which I use Obsidian and its tasks plugin. The other is the physical, which is a lined notebook I can carry with me. Each day in the morning I will pull tasks from my digital list and block out time to do them in my day. Throughout the day I write down new tasks onto my physical notebook in a designated section. Then at the end of the day, I take the tasks I completed, and mark them off in the digital notebook. Then I transfer the new tasks and their due dates into my digital tasks list.

Philosophy of Technology Use

Given my history with excessive technology use, this section is particular important. This is an area of my procedures that is in flux.

I do not use technology that does not improve my life. In practical terms this is all social media, all infotainment.

Procedure for technology use throughout the day. - If I am home, my phone is in the basket by the door, unless I am doing house chores, then I can play music on the upstairs speakers. - When I am out of the house I keep my phone in my backpack in the top pocket. I always have an e-reader/book with me. - When I am using a pc I should keep in mind my purpose in using that technology.
