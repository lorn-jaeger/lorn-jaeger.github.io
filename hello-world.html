Butte

I’m going back to Butte, where loneliness makes sense. I moved from Butte to Missoula for many sensible reasons: better school, more social opportunities, the ability to bike everywhere, Winco (which makes sense when all you have is a Walmart on the other side of town), not having to drive to see my family, and a warmer climate. But I still find myself missing it.

Some of this has to do with the fact that Butte was my first foray into adult life. I moved there just after graduation, when I was just 17. I had my first real apartment and job there, and I got married there. However, to me, it does not feel like these are the things that I miss about Butte.

In i’m going back to minnesota where sadness makes sense, Danez Smith expresses something I have been thinking about for a while. Similar to Minnesota, in many ways Butte looks like a bad place to live. There is a gaping hole in the mountains that's full of poison (Berkeley Pit), sometimes it gets down to -40 degrees in the winter, there are no jobs, and not much to do besides go to bars (not helpful if you don’t drink), and the programs in the college are weak. I think this fosters some sort of acceptance of your situation and invites the people living there to enjoy what they do have.

Missoula has a weird undercurrent to it; there is more focus on things being nice, whether that’s your clothes, your job, your car, your friends, or what you’re doing with your life. Maybe it’s all in my head. But I often think, even with all the things moving to Missoula got me, I would rather be in Butte, looking out the window in late May, with snow pouring down, and feeling a little lonely.
